About Me

B.S. Computer Science graduate and Educational Technology Master's student focused on accessibility, LMS support, and user-centered design.

My parents are Mr. and Mrs. Molina, and I am the oldest of twin siblings (boy and girl). Growing up, I was a student of disability and had a 504 plan that followed me from elementary school through college to now in university. Not once did I feel like I was treated differently or that my disability was a barrier to my success. I am grateful for the support and accommodations that allowed me to thrive academically and personally.

I went to San Martin De Porres Catholic School for elementary from first to third-grade; where my greatest achievement was completing mathematics at a fifth grade level in third-grade.

For the rest of elementary and middle school, I went to Horizon Montessory II in Weslaco,TX. I participated in club activities such as student council, where I served as vice-president in seventh grade. My academic achievemnts were being the top of my class with A-Honor Roll.

I then attended the Harlingen School of Health Professions for high school, where I was a member of the National Honor Society and completed over 90+ hours of clinical rotations in various healthcare settings, such as: Sports Medicine, Pediatrics, Radiology, and Physical Therapy (Out-Patient Rehab). My orignal goal was to work in the medical field, however, thanks to the COVID-19 pandemic, I had to pivot my career goals to something else.

Since the pandemic forced colleges and universities to switch to remote learning in 2020, I ended up taking all my core classes at South Texas College to see which career would catch my interest. I started in physical therapy, then changed to education, then art, then mathematics, until I eventually decided to pursue computer science after remembering a computer science elective I took in high school.

After my Associate's in 2023 at STC, I ended up enjoying computer science so much that I decided to pursue a Bachelor's in Computer Science at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley, where I was able to challenge myself furthur in programming and software development. There were so many electives I wanted to take, but unfortunately, my health took a turn and I had to speed up my graduation. My health worsened after coming back as an in-person student, this impacted my grades and affected my ability to retake any courses that I had C's in.
